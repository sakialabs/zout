## Lighting Direction

Lighting does the storytelling.

### General Rules

- Ball is always clearly lit
- Goal mouth slightly brighter than surroundings
- Backgrounds fall off into softness

### Environments

- **Street:** side lighting, harder shadows
- **Park:** even daylight, soft contrast
- **Cage:** enclosed light, tighter highlights
- **Stadium:** strong overhead lights, darker crowd

---

## Camera Language

The camera is a silent commentator.

### Default

- Calm
- Centered behind the ball
- Respectful distance

### Power Charge

- Subtle push-in
- No rotation

### Strike

- Micro shake (very small)
- No zoom

### Zout

- Tiny slow-motion beat
- Camera holds
- UI disappears briefly

### Top Bins

- Same as Zout
- Slight emphasis toward the corner
- One extra frame of hold

No spins.  
No whip pans.  
No showing off.

## Ball & Player

### Ball

The ball is the hero.

- Slightly larger than realistic for readability
- Clean texture with visible seams
- Always high contrast against background

### Player

- Minimal early on
- Legs or silhouette preferred
- Animation clarity > animation complexity

You donâ€™t need a full player model to feel football.

## Environments

Zout lives anywhere football is played.

### Streets

- Concrete, fences, rough edges
- Tight framing

### Parks

- Calm energy

### Cages

## UI Design

UI should **whisper**, not talk.

### Principles

- Thin lines
- Simple shapes
- Few elements at once
- Fade in, fade out

If players stare at UI, the design failed.

---

## Power & Timing UI

- Clean bars
- No sparks or particles
- Sweet spot subtly marked
- Centered and readable

UI should guide instinct, not demand attention.

---
